Effective Date: 1 January 2026  |  Last Updated: 1 January 2026

Your privacy is important to us. We respect your privacy and are committed to protecting it. To better protect your privacy, we provide this notice explaining our privacy practices and the choices available to you regarding how your information is collected and used.

This Privacy Policy is issued by Aldeia Realty - Real Estate, LDA, trading as Aldeia Realty, a company incorporated in Portugal under NIPC 517693032, with its registered office at Rua Dr. Angelo Ferrari, no. 7, R/C Dto., 2500-113 Caldas da Rainha, Portugal ("we", "our", or "us").

This policy complies with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), the California Consumer Privacy Act ("CCPA"), and other applicable privacy legislation.

1. What Type of Personal Information Do We Collect?

We may request personally identifiable information when you request a service, respond to a survey, enter a contest, correspond with us, or participate in certain other activities. You decide whether you wish to proceed with any activity that requests personal information. However, if you do not wish to provide the requested personal information, you may not be able to complete the transaction.

Similarly, when you visit our websites, we will collect information known as "clickstreams" (IP address, domain name, browser type, pages visited, etc.). This information, combined with the data you provide to us, allows us to better personalise your visits.

The categories of personal data we may collect include:

  • Identity data: name, surname
  • Contact data: email address, phone number, postal address
  • Preference data: property type, location preferences, budget range
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs
  • Usage data: pages visited, time spent, clicks, form submissions

2. How Do We Use the Information We Collect?

Under GDPR, we process your personal data on the following legal bases: (a) performance of a contract, (b) compliance with a legal obligation, (c) our legitimate interests, and (d) your consent.

We use your personal information in the following ways:

  • To provide you with information about property listings, market updates, newsletters, and informative emails;
  • To respond to your enquiries and provide the services you request (property searches, valuations, viewings);
  • To help us create content and services relevant to you;
  • To provide you with special offers that may be of interest to you, where you have consented to receive such communications;
  • To help us create better products and services that meet your needs;
  • To allow you to download documents, access services, or participate in other activities you select;
  • To comply with legal obligations, including anti-money laundering (AML) and tax regulations;
  • To analyse website traffic and improve user experience.

3. How Do We Protect Your Personal Information?

To ensure the security of your personal information, we have established a series of security measures and safeguards. Your personal information is stored on secure networks, which can only be accessed by a limited number of people who have special access rights and are committed to respecting and maintaining the confidentiality of such information.

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These include encryption of data in transit (SSL/TLS), access controls, and regular security assessments.

Despite these measures, when you provide personal information on the Internet there is always a risk that it may be intercepted and used by third parties beyond our control. Although we make every effort to protect your personal information and your privacy, we cannot guarantee the security of information you provide over the Internet.

4. Who Has Access to Your Personal Information and to What Extent?

In addition to companies within our group and for the purposes described above, we do not sell, exchange, or transfer your personal information to third parties in any way.

Your personal information may be transferred to external subcontractors to provide us with certain services (e.g., IT support, marketing services, legal compliance). It may be used to provide technical assistance. We require such subcontractors to keep our customers' personal information secure and confidential. We also require these suppliers to use personal information only on our behalf.

We may disclose your personal data where required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., a court or government agency).

You may at any time choose not to allow the sharing of your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes. Such a request can be made through our website in the contact section or by sending us an email.

5. International Data Transfers

As we serve international clients, your personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the European Economic Area ("EEA"). Where such transfers occur, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission.

6. Your Rights Under GDPR and CCPA

Depending on your location, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access — You have the right to request copies of your personal data.
  • Right to rectification — You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Right to erasure — You have the right to request that we erase your personal data, under certain conditions ("right to be forgotten").
  • Right to restrict processing — You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data.
  • Right to object — You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data.
  • Right to data portability — You have the right to request that we transfer the data we have collected to another organisation, or directly to you.
  • Right to withdraw consent — Where we rely on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time.
  • Right not to be subject to automated decision-making — Including profiling.

CCPA-specific rights (California residents only): You have the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete your personal information, the right to opt-out of the sale of personal information (note: we do not sell personal information), and the right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 10 below.

8. Cookies

This website uses a technology widely known in the industry as "cookies". A cookie is a unique text file that a website sends to your browser software. Cookies allow a website to personalise the information presented to you based on your browsing preferences.

We use cookies to personalise your pages, to remember your preferences, and to analyse website traffic. The information collected in this way helps us to optimise website content, navigation, and performance.

If you do not wish us to install cookies on your browser, you can set it to reject cookies. Rejecting cookies may affect your ability to use some of the services on our website.

9. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Typically, we retain personal data for a period of 5 years after the end of our business relationship, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

10. Options and Cancellation

We provide several ways for you to review, change, or delete your personal information, or to request that we not send you further information. We may also provide you with these options when you provide us with personal information.

You can review, update, or delete your personal information by contacting us directly. To protect your privacy and security, we will take reasonable steps to verify your identity before granting access or making corrections.
